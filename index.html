📌 SALES VS REVENUE — WHAT'S THE DIFFERENCE?
💰 SALES (SPA Signed)
When a buyer signs the Sale & Purchase Agreement (SPA). This is demand confirmed. The unit is officially sold.
📋 REVENUE (Recognised)
When construction milestones are certified complete. This is income earned. It progressively recognise from sales over 2-3 years.
💡 Why does this matter? The strong sales of RM2.0b in 2022 (SPA signed) are what drove the RM1.8b revenue peak in 2023. Today's sales are tomorrow's revenue. What the sales team closes today, your department delivers in the years ahead.

🔭 WHAT DOES 3,918 ACRES MEAN FOR YOU?
📅
A 13–16 year runway of work ahead
With a estimated future GDV of RM32 billion and LBS launching an average of RM2.0–2.4 billion in GDV every year, this landbank represents roughly 13 to 16 years of development work — a long, steady runway for everyone in the company. (Source: LBS IAR 2025)
📍
A landmark new project: Kwasa Damansara, RM8.3 billion GDV
In December 2025, LBS shareholders approved a major development in Kwasa Damansara — the "PJ West" area — with an estimated GDV of RM8.30 billion across 2,922 residential units. Beginning in 2027 and spanning 192.32 acres, this single project alone represents a decade of growth. (Source: LBS IAR 2025)
♻️
Landbank optimisation — recycling for sharper focus
LBS doesn't just acquire land — it actively manages the portfolio. In 2023, the Group continues to optimise our landbank, then replenished strategically through acquisitions and JV partnerships. This ongoing optimisation keeps the landbank productive, not idle. (Source: LBS IAR 2022–2025)
🏗️
How LBS builds faster: IBS is the competitive edge
LBS uses Industrialised Building System (IBS) technology across its projects — enabling faster construction, better quality control and fewer delays. In 2024, IBS-based development helped LBS deliver RM1.9 billion in completed project GDV despite a softer sales year. This is the engine that turns landbank into homes. (Source: LBS IAR 2024)
🔍 WHAT DOES THIS TELL US?

LBS' 3,918 acres landbank, with an estimated future GDV of RM32 billion, provides a strong 13–16 years development pipeline, supporting sustainable growth, strategic expansion, and long-term value creation.
